How to Prepare for the Canadian Citizenship Test

To become a Canadian Citizen, adults between the ages 18-54 years must pass the Canadian Citizenship test unless otherwise decided by Citizenship & Immigration Canada. If you take enough preparation, and properly, the test should not be too difficult. In fact, we hope you should be able to pass it in one go. Here are a few tips for the preparations:

  • There is an official study guide title 'Discover Canada: The Rights and Responsibilities of Citizenship' provided by 'Citizenship and Immigration Canada' from where all questions are asked in the test. If you can manage time, go through that book extensively and check your knowledge with the help of a friend or family member. Thera are 10 chapters in this guide. Please go through all the chapters thoroughly as questions may be generated from any of the chapter.
  • Reading just the guide as it is may not be enough for everyone. You need active learning to master the information properly as the questions are MCQ in nature. Only through active learning and practices the information will be registered in our mind.
  • Give special attention on questions which has numbers or dates associated with.
  • If multiple members of your family or friends are taking the test around same time, it is a good option to study and discuss together. Then you can ask questions to each other to check your progress.
